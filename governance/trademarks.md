# Trademarks and Certification: The Loom Method

> **Status: stub.** This states the policy direction and the marks it protects; the full trademark usage guidelines and certification-program terms will follow. Until then, if a use is not clearly permitted below, ask.

## The marks

- **The Loom Method** and **The Loom Manifesto** are trademarks of the project.
- **Note** and **Sett** are names of parts of The Loom Method, used as unregistered marks. Note is a methodology; Sett is both a methodology corpus and a standalone application, and a subsystem of the wider Loom system.
- A **certification mark** (for example, "Loom Certified") will be established for the conformance and certification program.

Trademarks are held by **Bruno Almeida do Lago**, to be transferred to a future **Loom Foundation** upon its incorporation, together with stewardship of The Loom Method.

## The method is open; the marks are not

The Loom Method is free to read, implement, teach, and fork under its open licenses. Each part carries the license that fits it:

- **The Loom Manifesto**: CC BY-ND 4.0. Shareable verbatim, not adapted.
- **The methodology and its corpora** (Note, Sett's corpus, and others to come): CC BY 4.0.
- **The applications** (Sett, and further tools to come): each under its own open-source license. Sett's source code is MIT OR Apache-2.0.

None of these licenses grants any trademark right (Apache-2.0 §6; the CC and MIT licenses are silent on marks). Copyright and patents are handled by the licenses and the patent non-assertion pledge ([`patents.md`](patents.md)); the marks are governed here.

This separation is deliberate. Copyright cannot protect a method, and the project does not want it to: anyone may practise and build on Loom. What the marks protect is **provenance and conformance**: the assurance that something presented as "The Loom Method" or as "Loom Certified" is what it claims to be.

## What you may do

- Implement Note, run and fork Sett, and teach or write about The Loom Method, freely and without permission.
- State truthfully that your work uses or is compatible with Loom. For example, "built with Note", "works with Sett", or "based on The Loom Method", as an accurate factual reference (nominative fair use), without implying endorsement or certification.

## What requires authorization

- Using the marks as the name or branding of your own product, tool, distribution, or fork, or in any way that implies the Foundation endorses, sponsors, or produces it.
- Claiming that an implementation, corpus, course, or credential **is** The Loom Method, is official, or is **conformant** or **certified**, other than through the Foundation's certification program.

## Certification and conformance

The Foundation runs, and is the sole authority for, the certification and conformance program: training, examinations, credentials, and the conformance mark. This program, together with the marks, is how the Foundation funds its stewardship. The corpus and method stay open, while the official credential and the right to use the marks come only from the Foundation. Anyone may offer training or tooling for Loom; only the Foundation certifies it.
